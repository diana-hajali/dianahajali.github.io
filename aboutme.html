ABOUT ME

Diana Hajali graduates from the Bush School of Government and Public Service with a master's degree in international affairs on May 7, 2020. Her degree track focuses on national security and diplomacy, with concentrations in intelligence and European studies. Diana also completed a bachelor's degree in international studies focusing on politics and diplomacy, and a minor in French, at Texas A&M University in 2015.


Diana was born in Beirut, Lebanon and immigrated to the United States with her family in 2000. She has traveled to various countries around the world, stayed in a refugee camp, and completed a study abroad program in Grenoble, France. Diana knows advanced Arabic and French and has some experience with C++ and web design.


In graduate school, Diana has taken courses on diplomacy, foreign policy, intelligence analysis, counterintelligence, and terrorism, among others. Through her studies, Diana has learned how to effectively use Structured Analytic Techniques and other Advanced Analytic Approaches to produce intelligence analytical products. Her most significant project included contributing to a final report on terrorists' use of cryptocurrency for the National Security Agency using open source intelligence. Some of Diana's other skills include public speaking, which she enhanced during her time as president of the Texas A&M Speech and Debate Team, communication, critical thinking, researching, and leadership. Diana is currently seeking a full-time job working in either the federal government or private sector.
