ABOUT ME

Diana Hajali is currently an International Trade Specialist in the Office of Standards & Intellectual Property at the U.S. Department of Commerce's International Trade Administration. In her role, Diana focuses on technical barriers to trade facing U.S. industry in addition to the development of U.S. standards policy and its promulgation in bilateral and multilateral trade policy settings. Her portfolio includes the Middle East and North Africa region, the European Union, and the Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation (APEC), and covers a variety of industries, more recently specific to emerging technologies such as artificial intelligence (AI) and climate-related technologies. Diana is a Certified Global Business Professional (CGBP) and has completed a Certificate in AI through the Executive Branch Artificial Intelligence Lab, a Wilson Center Science & Technology Innovation Program.


Diana graduated from the Bush School of Government and Public Service with a master's degree in international affairs on May 7, 2020. Her degree track focused on national security and diplomacy, with concentrations in intelligence and European studies. Diana also completed a bachelor's degree in international studies focusing on politics and diplomacy, and a minor in French, at Texas A&M University in 2015. Diana has traveled to various countries around the world and completed a study abroad program in Grenoble, France in 2017. Diana knows advanced Arabic and intermediate French.


Some of Diana's skills include public speaking, which she enhanced during her time as president of the Texas A&M Speech and Debate Team, communication, program management, and leadership. Diana has a passion for cross-cultural exchange programs, global trade, trade event management, immigration, and anything that has to do with helping others. In her free time, Diana enjoys reading, playing video games, going to the gym, and baking.
