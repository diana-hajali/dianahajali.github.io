ABOUT ME

Diana Hajali graduated from the Bush School of Government and Public Service with a master's degree in international affairs on May 7, 2020. Her degree track focused on national security and diplomacy, with concentrations in intelligence and European studies. Diana also completed a bachelor's degree in international studies focusing on politics and diplomacy, and a minor in French, at Texas A&M University in 2015.


Diana was born in Beirut, Lebanon and immigrated to the United States with her family in 2000. She has traveled to various countries around the world, stayed in a refugee camp, and completed a study abroad program in Grenoble, France. Diana knows advanced Arabic and French and has some experience with C++ and web design.


In graduate school, Diana took courses on diplomacy, foreign policy, intelligence analysis, international development, and the Middle East, among others. Some of Diana's skills include public speaking, which she enhanced during her time as president of the Texas A&M Speech and Debate Team, communication, research, and leadership. Diana has a passion for cross-cultural exchange programs, higher education, refugees, immigration, study abroad and international student assistance, and anything that has to do with working with people and helping others. Diana is currently seeking a full-time position working in either the federal government or private sector.
